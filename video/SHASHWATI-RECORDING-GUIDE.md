# Shashwati — Voiceover Recording Guide

## Setup
- Quiet room, no echo
- Phone voice memo or laptop mic — hold steady, close to mouth
- Speak naturally, with energy — this is a pitch, not a lecture
- Pace: confident, not rushed. ~150 words per minute.
- Save each file as MP3 or M4A

## Scripts

### S01 — Sarah's Story (Cold Open) ~25s
> This is Sarah.
> She's 34, she lives in Liverpool, and she's partially sighted.
> This morning, she wants to order birthday flowers for her mum.
> She finds a local florist online — Bloom and Petal. Beautiful website.
> But when her screen reader hits the checkout button… nothing happens.
> No label. No focus. The page just stops.
> Sarah tries three more times. Then she closes the tab.

**Tone:** Storytelling. Warm, gentle, drawing the listener in. Slight pause after "nothing happens" for effect.

---

### S02 — The Problem ~20s
> Sarah isn't alone.
> 94.8% of the top million websites have accessibility failures.
> That's 51 errors per homepage.
> And in the UK, 24% of the population — 16 million people — are now disabled.
> Most businesses have no idea their websites are shutting them out.

**Tone:** Serious, factual. Let the numbers land. Slight emphasis on "16 million" and "shutting them out."

---

### S03 — The Money & The Law ~20s
> Those 16 million people control 274 billion pounds a year in spending.
> 7 in 10 of them click away from sites they can't use.
> And here's what most business owners don't realise — this isn't optional.
> The Equality Act 2010 makes it a legal obligation.
> The European Accessibility Act is now in force.
> This isn't coming. It's here.

**Tone:** Building urgency. Start factual, build to the punch — "This isn't coming. It's here." should land with conviction.

---

### S06 — Customers & Pricing ~20s
> We built this for UK SMEs — 5 to 50 employees, businesses that have never heard of WCAG.
> A free scan gives them their score and top 3 issues.
> Full report: 29 to 49 pounds. Video walkthrough: 99 pounds.
> And our monitoring agent checks weekly for 19 pounds a month — recurring revenue.
> 100 subscribers gets us to 22,800 pounds ARR.

**Tone:** Confident, commercial. You're showing the business model works. Emphasise "recurring revenue" and the ARR figure.

---

### S08 — Your Intro ~6s
> I'm Shashwati — MSc Advanced Marketing.
> I design the audit methodology, the reports, and the go-to-market.

**Tone:** Confident, personal. Own it. Vedant's part follows immediately after yours.

---

### S09 — The Plan & The Ask ~20s
> We're asking for 5,000 pounds.
> 500 for user research with disabled testers. 1,000 for marketing. 400 for AI infrastructure.
> The rest for design, legal, and workshops.
> April: build the MVP. May: pilot with 20 Liverpool SMEs.
> By September — 100 customers, 30 monitoring subscribers, and over 5,000 pounds recovered.
> At 100 subscribers, that's 22,800 pounds ARR.

**Tone:** Clear and structured. The judges want to see you know where every pound goes. Be precise on the numbers, confident on the timeline.

---

### S10 — The Flowers Arrive (Closing) ~20s
> Three weeks later, the florist at Bloom and Petal runs a NeuroEdge audit.
> Scores 34 out of 100. Fixes 23 issues — including that checkout button.
> Sarah visits again.
> This time, the flowers arrive on her mum's birthday.
> Every audit we sell is a barrier removed, a customer unlocked, and a legal risk reduced.
> This is NeuroEdge.

**Tone:** Full circle. Warm storytelling returning to Sarah. Build emotion through "the flowers arrive." Final line — "This is NeuroEdge" — should feel like a mic drop. Proud, certain.

---

## Tips
- Do a practice run of all 7 scripts before recording
- It's OK to do multiple takes — send your best one for each
- Leave ~1 second of silence at the start and end of each file
- Total recording time: ~2-3 minutes of audio
