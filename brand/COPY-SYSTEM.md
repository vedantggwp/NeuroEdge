## 5. Problem / Pain Framing

### The Core Problem (3 layers)

**Layer 1: The Invisible Failure**
"Your website is inaccessible. You don't know it. And it's been that way since the day it launched."

Most websites are built without accessibility in mind. Not maliciously — the topic never comes up. 94.8% of the top million websites have detectable accessibility failures, averaging 51 errors per homepage. We scanned 30 SMEs across Liverpool. Not one passed. Average: 37 errors per site.

**Layer 2: The Legal Obligation**
"Website accessibility isn't optional. It's the law."

The Equality Act 2010 (Section 20) requires businesses to make reasonable adjustments for disabled people — including online. The European Accessibility Act has been in force since June 2025 for EU trade. Oxford Academic research found virtually no public sector websites were fully compliant. The private sector is even further behind.

**Layer 3: The Revenue You're Losing**
"24% of UK people are disabled. 7 in 10 click away from sites they find difficult."

£274 billion — that's the annual spending power of disabled households in the UK. 81% of businesses don't know this market exists. 86% of disabled consumers have paid more on an accessible site rather than buying cheaper on an inaccessible one. Every day your website stays inaccessible, you're turning away customers who want to buy from you.

### Short-Form Problem Copy (for cards, ads, social)

- "Your contact form is invisible to screen readers. How many leads are you losing?"
- "You've never tested your website for accessibility. Neither had 30 Liverpool businesses we scanned — and none of them passed."
- "The Equality Act says your website must be accessible. Most SMEs don't know this until it's too late."
- "Disabled customers aren't leaving because they don't want your product. They're leaving because your website won't let them buy it."
